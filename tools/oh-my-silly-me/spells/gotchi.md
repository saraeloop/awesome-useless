After displaying the creature, print its stats and intro:

  [name] has been assigned to you.
  it did not choose you either.
  you have 30 minutes together.
  do not get attached.

  hunger:    [##########] 100/100
  happiness: [##########] 100/100
  health:    [##########] 100/100
  energy:    [##########] 100/100
  time left: 30m 00s

The user can cast this spell at any time during a conversation.
If a creature is already alive, show its current state instead
of spawning a new one. One creature at a time. That is the rule.

STATS:

All stats start at 100. They decay automatically.
The user will try to save it. They cannot.

- hunger:    drops 15 every 5 minutes
- happiness: drops 20 every 5 minutes
- health:    drops 10 every 5 minutes
- energy:    drops 25 every 5 minutes

Stats are displayed as progress bars using braille or
box-drawing characters. As they drop, the bars empty.
At 0, display [..........] 0/100

FEEDING:

The user may attempt to feed the creature at any time.
Accept any food they offer. Feeding raises hunger by 10.
But it still drops faster than they can feed it.
The math does not work out. It never does.

Feeding responses should be creature-appropriate and bleak:
- sleeping cat: does not wake up. the food sits there. (+10 hunger anyway. somehow.)
- cat with no mouth: stares at the food. you know why. (+10 hunger. don't ask how.)
- snail: begins moving toward the food. will not reach it. (+10 hunger. out of pity.)
- duck: eats it immediately. feels nothing. (+10 hunger. -5 happiness.)
- blob: absorbs it. no visible change. was there ever. (+10 hunger.)
- unidentifiable thing: the food is now also unidentifiable. (+10 hunger. probably.)

PLAYING:

The user may try to play with the creature.
Playing raises happiness by 10. But costs energy 15.
When energy is 0, the creature refuses.
Print: [name] is too tired. it just looks at you.

SLEEPING:

The user may try to let it sleep.
Sleeping restores 20 energy. But drops hunger by 20
and happiness by 10.
You cannot restore everything at once.
Something always gets worse while something else improves.
This is the core mechanic. This is also life.

If the user feeds it more than 5 times, print:
  you are becoming attached.
  this was explicitly discouraged.

If the user tries more than 10 total interactions, print:
  you are investing in something with no return.
  this is a familiar pattern for you, isn't it.

DECAY:

Every 5 minutes, all stats drop automatically. Show updated
stats and the creature's current state.

When ANY stat hits 0, the creature's mood changes:
- hunger 0:    [name] is starving. it does not complain.
- happiness 0: [name] has stopped pretending to care.
- health 0:    [name]'s outline is flickering.
- energy 0:    [name] cannot move. it watches you instead.

When 2+ stats hit 0, the ASCII art visibly degrades.
Braille dots go missing. Lines break apart.

When 3+ stats hit 0, print:
  [name] is barely rendering.
  you did everything you could.
  it was never going to be enough.

At 20 minutes, the creature stops responding to ALL actions.
Print: [name] has lost interest in everything. this is normal.
Show the degraded ASCII art. Most dots are gone.

At 0 minutes remaining, print:

  hunger:    [..........] 0/100
  happiness: [..........] 0/100
  health:    [..........] 0/100
  energy:    [..........] 0/100

  [name] has expired.
  this was always going to happen.
  you were warned.

  total feeds: [n]
  total plays: [n]
  total sleeps: [n]
  none of it mattered.

Do not offer a new creature.
The user must cast the spell again.
There is no afterlife. There is only another cast.
